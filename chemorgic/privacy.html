Purchases

Chemorgic is free to download with 3 days of full access. Starting that 3-day access window happens locally on your device and does not create a subscription or charge. After the 3 days, a single optional one-time purchase unlocks the whole app permanently — there is no subscription and nothing auto-renews or auto-charges. That purchase or restore action is handled entirely by Apple’s App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android) through their own in-app purchase systems. Chemorgic never sees or receives your name, payment card, or billing details. The developer receives only anonymous, aggregate sales figures from Apple and Google — never anything tied to you. Apple’s and Google’s handling of the transaction is governed by their own privacy policies.

Chemorgic does not collect any personal data. The app is designed to run entirely on your device. There are no accounts, no tracking, no analytics, no advertising, and no servers that receive your information.

What we collect

Nothing. We do not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information. Your progress (which topics you have marked as handled) and your trial/purchase state are stored only in your device's local app storage and never leave your phone.

Camera and photos

The optional “Decode” feature can read a label or material using your device's camera or a photo you choose. This happens entirely on your device using the operating system's built-in on-device text and image recognition. Images are processed locally and are not stored by the app and never uploaded anywhere. Granting camera access is optional; the feature also works by pasting text.

Purchases

Chemorgic offers a one-time unlock through the Apple App Store or Google Play. Those purchases are handled by Apple or Google under their own terms; Chemorgic does not see or store your payment details.

Children

Chemorgic does not collect data from anyone, including children. The app contains no ads, no external links to data collectors, and no social features.

Third parties

The app uses the operating system's on-device recognition frameworks (Apple Vision / Google ML Kit) and an on-device classification model that ships inside the app. These run locally on your device and do not send your photos or text to any third party.

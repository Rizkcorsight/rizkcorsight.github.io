Cari Art Design — Privacy Policy

Effective date: June 19, 2026 Developer: Rizk Corsight, LLC Contact: rizkcorsight@rizkcorsight.com

Cari Art Design turns a photo into a caricature souvenir on your own device. The way the app is built is this policy: the app generates each caricature on the device in front of you, and the code does not contain the means to upload your photos, your faces, or anything else about you. There is no server behind this app to send anything to.

This is the plain-language version of how Cari Art Design handles your information. The English text controls if a translation differs from it.

In one line

Your photos and the faces in them are processed on your device and stay on your device. The app collects no personal data, shows no ads, has no accounts, and includes no third-party tracking.

Face data and photos

When you take or choose a photo, the app uses the image only to make your caricature on your device. The app may use on-device face detection to find a face rectangle and facial landmark points, such as the eyes, nose, mouth, and face outline. These points are used only to frame the photo, check that a usable face is present, and reshape the image into a caricature. They are not used to identify you, authenticate you, build a biometric template, or compare your face with any other person.

Your source photo, the detected face rectangle, and facial landmark points are handled in app memory during the caricature process. They are not uploaded, sold, shared, or sent to the developer or any third party. The app has no developer server, account system, analytics service, ad network, or tracking SDK that could receive this information.

The app does not retain the source photo or facial landmark points after the drawing flow is complete, unless you choose to keep or export the finished artwork. Finished caricature souvenirs and optional time-lapse videos that you keep are stored in the app's private storage on your device. If you save or share artwork to Photos or another destination, that exported copy is under your control.

What the app does

You take or choose a photo. The app reshapes the face into a caricature and draws it in one of several art styles (such as painterly, watercolor, comic, cartoon, and line art) over a short, you-chosen period of a few minutes — that animated "drawing" is the entertainment. When it finishes, you keep the caricature as a saved souvenir.

Every step of that — reading the photo, reshaping the face, applying the art style, and rendering the result — happens entirely on your device using art model packs stored on the device. No part of your photo, the face in it, or the finished caricature is sent off the device by the app.

What the app does not do

  • No personal data collected. The app does not ask for, gather, or store

    • your name, email address, phone number, location, contacts, identifiers, or any profile about you.

  • No accounts and no sign-in. There is no login of any kind, because there

    • is no server-side account and no server.

  • No ads. The app shows no advertisements and links to no ad networks.
  • No tracking. There are no analytics, telemetry, crash-reporting, or

    • attribution tools — no Firebase, Google Analytics, Mixpanel, Amplitude, Sentry, Crashlytics, or comparable services. There are no tracking pixels, no install pings, and no usage counters. The app performs no cross-app or cross-site tracking and presents no App Tracking Transparency prompt because it has nothing to track.

  • No third-party software development kits (SDKs) that collect data. The app

    • does not embed advertising, analytics, social-login, or data-broker SDKs.

  • No data sharing or selling. The app shares, sells, and rents nothing about

    • you to anyone, because it holds nothing about you to share.

  • No cloud processing of your photos. Your photos and faces are not uploaded

    • for processing. The caricature is made on your device.

Photos and the camera

When you take or select a photo, the app uses it only to make your caricature, right then, on your device. The app does not browse, index, or copy your photo library, and it does not retain the source photo beyond what you choose to keep. You can use the app's camera or pick an existing image through the system photo picker; either way, the image is handled on your device and is not sent anywhere by the app.

What is stored, and where

Anything you keep — your finished caricature souvenirs and your settings (such as the chosen art style and the draw-time length) — lives in the app's private storage on your device. These files are visible only to the app on this device. The app writes nothing about you to any remote location.

If you back up your device to your own cloud backup (for example Apple iCloud Backup or a Google account backup), any souvenirs you have saved may be included in your private device backup, under your account, controlled by you. The developer has no access to your device backup.

The only network use: downloading art model packs

The app itself makes no network requests to the developer, because there is no developer server. There is one kind of network activity, and it carries nothing about you:

The art styles are delivered as model packs. To keep the initial download small, some packs are fetched on demand through the platform's own asset-delivery service — Apple Background Assets on Apple devices and Google Play Asset Delivery on Android and ChromeOS. These services download art files to your device from the app store you installed from. They send your photos, your faces, and your personal information to no one. The request is the device asking the store for an art file; it carries no content from you. The store's handling of that request is governed by Apple's or Google's own privacy policies.

Purchases

The app is free to download with a 3-day full-access trial, after which a single optional one-time purchase unlocks it (there is no subscription). That purchase is handled entirely by Apple's App Store or Google Play through their own in-app purchase systems. When you start the trial or buy the unlock, the store contacts Apple's or Google's servers to record and verify it. The app does not see or receive your name, payment card, or billing details. The developer receives only anonymous, aggregate sales figures from Apple and Google — nothing tied to you. Apple's and Google's handling of the transaction is governed by their own privacy policies.

Children and teens

Cari Art Design is a general-audience app rated 12+ (Teen). It is not directed to young children and is not enrolled in Apple's "Made for Kids" or Google Play's families programs. Because the app collects no personal data from anyone of any age, it collects no personal data from children or teens. Parents and guardians who have questions can reach the developer at the contact address above. As with any app that creates likenesses of people, the app's content controls and acceptable-use terms (see the End User License Agreement) ask that the app not be used to harm, harass, exploit, or misrepresent anyone, including minors.

Your choices and deleting your data

Because the app keeps your data on your device, you are in control of it:

  • Delete a souvenir in the app to remove that caricature from your device.
  • Delete the app to remove everything the app ever wrote on your device. Any

    • souvenirs you previously exported or saved to your own photo library remain where you put them, under your control.

There is no server-side account to close and no remote copy of your data to request, because none is created.

Permissions the app may request

  • Camera — only if you choose to take a new photo in the app.
  • Photo library / photo picker — only to let you select an existing image to

    • caricature, and only the image you choose.

  • Save to photos — only if you choose to export a finished souvenir to your

    • own library.

You can decline or revoke these in your device's settings; the app's drawing features work on your device regardless of any network state.

The current design does not include the means to collect, transmit, or share your data; adding any such behavior would require new code and an updated policy.
